Description

Shahi Paneer is a rich and royal Indian curry made with soft paneer (cottage cheese) cubes cooked in a creamy, flavorful gravy. The dish features a delicate blend of aromatic spices, ground nuts, and a luxurious touch of cream that creates a smooth and indulgent sauce. Traditionally served in royal kitchens, Shahi Paneer is a perfect balance of savory and slightly sweet flavors, making it a beloved dish at special occasions and celebrations. Whether served with naan, roti, or rice, it is sure to impress anyone who enjoys authentic Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer (cottage cheese), cut into cubes
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts
  • 1/4 cup melon seeds (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons poppy seeds
  • 1 tablespoon melon seeds (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1-2 green cardamom pods, powdered
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, for slight sweetness)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Recipe

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee in a pan and sauté the finely chopped onion until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add the pureed tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the oil separates from the masala.
  4. Grind the cashew nuts, melon seeds, and poppy seeds into a smooth paste and add it to the pan. Stir well and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Now add the paneer cubes and mix gently to coat them with the gravy.
  6. Pour in the fresh cream and simmer for another 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend together.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
